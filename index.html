Project Overview

I led the development of a digital system to improve how defect reports are captured, structured, and managed in social housing ‚Äî ensuring compliance with new regulatory requirements.

üéØ The Challenge

Social housing providers face increasing regulatory pressure to respond quickly and systematically to defect reports. Traditional manual processes struggle with consistency, traceability, and the documentation requirements of new legislation.

  • Variable quality of incoming defect reports
  • Difficulty maintaining consistent record-keeping
  • Compliance timelines requiring rapid response
  • Need for full audit trails and evidence management

Regulatory Context

Understanding the regulatory landscape that shaped this project's requirements.

üìã Why This Matters

Awaab's Law (Social Housing Regulation Act 2023) mandates that social housing providers must acknowledge defect reports within 24 hours and investigate hazards within 14 days. This creates an urgent need for systems that can capture, structure, and track defect reports with full audit trails.

üèõÔ∏è Building Safety Act 2022

Introduces the "Golden Thread" requirement ‚Äî digital records of building information that must be accessible and maintained throughout a building's lifecycle.

‚ö†Ô∏è Awaab's Law 2023

24-hour acknowledgment, 14-day investigation timescales. Requires structured evidence capture and traceable response timelines.

üîß Decent Homes Standard

Updated requirements for property condition. Systematic defect tracking becomes essential for demonstrating compliance.

üìä HHSRS Assessments

Housing Health and Safety Rating System requires consistent hazard identification and documentation across property portfolios.

Why this matters for structural engineering: Defect management is no longer just a maintenance issue ‚Äî it's a compliance requirement with legal consequences. Engineers who can bridge the gap between traditional practice and digital workflows will lead the profession's response to these new requirements.

Key Learnings

Insight

AI Works Best with Clear Boundaries

AI is most effective when given specific, well-defined tasks rather than broad responsibilities. Clear separation between AI assistance and professional decision-making is essential.

Insight

Starting with regulatory requirements ‚Äî rather than retrofitting compliance ‚Äî results in more robust systems that naturally support audit and evidence needs.

Insight

Structural engineers who understand both domain requirements and technology capabilities are uniquely positioned to drive meaningful digital transformation in practice.

Insight
