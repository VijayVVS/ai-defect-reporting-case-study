Overview

Project Overview

I led the design and development of a digital system to improve how defect reports are captured, structured, and tracked in social housing — with Awaab's Law compliance built in from the start.

The Problem

Social housing providers are under growing regulatory pressure to respond to defect reports quickly and with a clear paper trail. Manual processes make this difficult — reports come in inconsistently, records are hard to trace, and compliance timelines are tight.

  • Variable quality of incoming defect reports
  • Inconsistent record-keeping across properties
  • Tight compliance timelines under new legislation
  • Need for audit trails and evidence management

What I Built

A digital tool that uses AI to structure incoming reports while keeping all engineering decisions with the professional. The system handles data capture, classification, and compliance tracking — the engineer handles judgement.

  • AI-assisted data extraction and structuring
  • Compliance timelines and tracking built in
  • Full audit trail for every report
  • Clear separation between AI suggestions and professional decisions

Why This Matters

Structural engineers don't typically build software. But the problems this system solves — consistency, traceability, speed of response — are engineering problems. Understanding both the regulatory requirements and the technology meant I could build something that actually works in practice, rather than a generic IT solution imposed from outside the profession.

Regulatory Context

Regulatory Context

The legislation that shaped this project's requirements.

Why This Matters

Awaab's Law (Social Housing Regulation Act 2023) requires social housing providers to acknowledge defect reports within 24 hours and investigate hazards within 14 days. That means systems need to capture, structure, and track reports with full audit trails — there's no room for lost paperwork.

Building Safety Act 2022

Introduces the "Golden Thread" — digital records of building information that must be accessible and maintained throughout a building's lifecycle.

Awaab's Law 2023

24-hour acknowledgment, 14-day investigation timescales. Requires structured evidence capture and traceable response timelines.

Decent Homes Standard

Updated requirements for property condition. Systematic defect tracking is now essential for demonstrating compliance.

HHSRS Assessments

Housing Health and Safety Rating System requires consistent hazard identification and documentation across property portfolios.

Why this matters for structural engineering: Defect management is no longer just maintenance — it carries legal consequences. Engineers who can work across traditional practice and digital systems will be the ones shaping the profession's response.

Takeaways

What I Learned

Practical takeaways from applying AI and software development to engineering practice.

// Lesson 01

AI Needs Clear Boundaries

AI works best on specific, well-defined tasks. Give it broad responsibilities and the output gets unreliable. The clearest gains came from keeping AI on data capture and leaving decisions to the engineer.

// Lesson 02

Start with Compliance, Not Features

Building compliance in from the start produced a more robust system than trying to retrofit it. The regulatory requirements actually made the design better, not more complicated.

// Lesson 03

Engineers Should Lead Digital Change

Engineers who understand both the domain and the technology can build tools that actually get used. Generic IT solutions imposed from outside rarely fit how engineers work.

// Lesson 04

Build for the Actual Users

The best technical solution is useless if people won't use it. I had to design for both tenants reporting defects and engineers triaging them — two very different user groups.

Reflection

This project confirmed that technology works best when it serves the workflow rather than trying to own it. The real value is in enabling better practice — faster triage, consistent data, proper evidence handling — while keeping accountability where it belongs: with the engineer.
